      Help Center

      You’re in the app. This guide is for using the tracker while signed in. Before you sign in, use the site’s Help page and FAQs for quick answers—each covers different ground so you’re not reading the same text twice. No cloud sync in this version.

      Start here

      First session

      1. Guest or account: Try Continue as guest instantly, or create a username—data stays in this browser.
      2. Targets: Open Goals and set calories plus macros (protein, carbs, fat, sugar, vegetables).
      3. Logging: Use Log Food for search, meal type, and past dates. Save usual meals on Profile for one-tap fill.
      4. Insights: Open Dashboard for rings, trends, and weight over time.

      Tip: Weigh in at a consistent time (for example, morning, before eating) for smoother trend lines.

      Dashboard

      Rings & charts

      Six rings summarize calories, protein, carbs, fat, sugar, and vegetable servings (1 cup ≈ 90 g). Charts support filters and fullscreen; tap or hover points for values.

      Tip: A green halo means you’ve met or passed that day’s target—use it as feedback, not a rule.

      Trends & Insights (below your rings) includes Personalized insights from your meal names, macros vs goals, and Profile (eating pattern, notes, usual meals), plus charts for calories, macros, patterns, food groups, and weight—general feedback, not medical advice.

      Food search

      Database & manual entry

      Search loads from the bundled database (metadata reports 15,000+ foods). Pick an item to fill nutrition fields; many vegetables auto-suggest a cup serving. If something isn’t listed, enter numbers manually and still pick meal type and date.

      Goals (science-friendly)

      Calories & macros

      • Weight change: Small, steady deficits or surpluses (often discussed around 300–500 kcal/day) are commonly used; a clinician or dietitian can personalize this.
      • Protein: Many adults land near roughly 0.8–1.2 g/kg body weight; athletes may go higher.
      • Carbs / fat: Broad guidelines often cite ~45–65% carbs and ~20–35% fat of total energy—tune to what you tolerate and enjoy.
      • Added sugar: AHA cites about 25 g/day (women) and 36 g/day (men) as upper limits for added sugar.
      • Produce: Public-health messaging often encourages multiple cup-equivalents of vegetables daily; your target in the app is yours to set.

      Medical note: CaloriEat is a self-tracking tool, not medical advice. For conditions like diabetes, eating disorders, or pregnancy, work with a licensed professional.

      Privacy

      Your data stays local

      • Profiles, meals, and weigh-ins live in localStorage on this device.
      • No cloud backup here—clearing site data or switching browsers starts fresh unless you export elsewhere yourself.
      • After the first load, core screens work offline; Chart.js may cache from CDN depending on browser.

      Food category colors

      How items are tagged

      • Fruits Fruit and typical fruit dishes
      • Veggies Vegetables and salads
      • Whole grains Oats, brown rice, whole wheat
      • Lean proteins Poultry, fish, tofu, legumes
      • Processed Fast food, deli, many packaged items
      • Sugary Sweets, sugary drinks, desserts

      Troubleshooting

      Common fixes

      • Nothing saves: Allow storage for this site; private mode may clear on exit.
      • Charts blank: Reload once; confirm network for the Chart.js script if it didn’t cache.
      • Search empty: Wait for the database to finish loading, then try a shorter query.
      • Rings look wrong: Confirm today’s date on logged meals and that goals match under Goals.
